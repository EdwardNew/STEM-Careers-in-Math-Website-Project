Data Analyst

Description: Data analysts translate numbers into readable English. Their main job is to take the companies data and use it to make better decisions. They also figure out the price for projects to see whether it will fit in the set budget.

  • Education Required: Bachelor's degree in entry-level jobs and a Master's degree for upper-level jobs in fields like statistics, math, computer science, etc.
  • Training (What you need to learn): You should be able to analyze data, communicate, use critical thinking, and be strong in the field of math
  • Experience (Any job experience prior?): program well in one or more programming language
  • Annual Pay: $81,000 (Varies between different positions)
  • Job Trend: 27% increase between 2016-2026
  • Emerging: Data analysts are growing in high demands by many businesses because they are needed to manage huge amounts of data within companies and businesses.
  • Threatened by AI?: The job of a data analyst requires lots of critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Even though there are some portions of the job that an AI could replace, most parts of a data analyst that relate to solving issues and communicating problems is irreplaceable.
