Financial Consultant

Description: A financial consultant works with companies or individuals to plan the financial status by offering guidance on topics that include investments, taxes, and many financial decisions. They work closely with their client and give personal advice relating to the situation. Their main jobs is to make sure the company is stable financially and give advice to keep it that way.

  • Education Required: Bachelor's degree in finance, economics, business, statistics, or any similar fields. Get a certificate normally after 3 years of work experience. Some could end up pursuing an MBA after.
  • Certificates:
    • Certified Financial Planner (CFP)
    • Personal Financial Specialist (PFS)
    • Chartered Financial Consultant (ChFC)
    • Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA)
  • Training (What you need to learn): Classes to keep updated with all the current financial trends.
  • Experience (Any job experience prior?): Serving as an intern in financial firms to apply what they've learned
  • Pay: Mean Annual Wage:$108,000
  • Job Trend: 27% increase by 2022
  • Emerging: Financial advisors are one of the toughest jobs to fill. As the population ages and life expectancies rise, demand for financial planning will rise.
  • Threatened by AI?: They will not be replaced by AI because AI is programmed to think in a certain way, but the job requires you to be versatile in your thinking. Humans can think and use reasoning while robots can't.
