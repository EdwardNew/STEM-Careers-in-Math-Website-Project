Stock Broker

Description: A stock broker connects the buyers and the sellers of numerous stocks to ensure a proficient market. They also tarde on behalf of companies and take a percentage of the money earned from the stocks. They also advise their clients whether to buy, sell of hold their stocks to ensure the most profit. They typically work alone.

  • Education Required: Bachelor's degree in anything but something in business or financial field recommended.
  • Training (What you need to learn): An individual must be sponsored by FINRA(Financial Industry Regulatory Authority) and take classes in order to keep their licenses renewed.
  • Experience (Any job experience prior?): Intern at insurance firms or brokage firms
  • Annual Pay: $64,000
  • Job Trend: 6% increase in between 2016-2026
  • Threatened by AI?: Stock broker does not seem to look like it would be replaced by AI because it requires communication between both sides and AI would not be able to show the emotion and critical thinking for it to work the job properly.
