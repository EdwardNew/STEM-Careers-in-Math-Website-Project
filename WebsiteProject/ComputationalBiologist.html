Computatuional Biologist

Description: Computational Biologists use biological data to develop formulas and systems in order to help understand biological systems and relationships. In order to do this, they utilize statistics, computer science, and mathematics in order to help solve biological problems-- mostly concerned with the analysis of things such as DNA, RNA, and other biosequences.

  • Education Required: Bachelor's degree sufficient for some positions with federal government; PH.D. required for private sector
  • Training (What you need to learn): Biomedical sciences, mathematics, computer science or related fields
  • Experience (Any job experience prior?): 3-5 years of experience
  • Pay: Median annual wage of $110,620
  • Job Trend: Projected to grow by 18% in between 2014-2024
  • Emerging: This career is emerging due to the skill needed. Companies are starting to offer more official training for newer computational biologists.
  • Threatened by AI?: A computational biologist can be threatened by AI. AI can compute calculations much faster than any human, and can also more quickly use logic to assess the experiment and analyze the results, therefore doing the job much better and more efficiently than any human.
