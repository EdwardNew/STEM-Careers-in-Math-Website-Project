Actuarial Analyst

Description: An actuarial analysts works with insurance industry's to use statistical models to calculate the probability of cost associated with certain tasks and situations such as product failure, property damage, injury, and even death. They use this information to calculate the price of insurance policies.

  • Education Required: Bachelors or Masters degree in statistics or any related degrees. Either Society of Actuaries(SOA) or Casualty Actuarial Society(CAS) must certify actuarial analysts. Needs to achieve an associate level of certification by taking 7 tests in CAS or 5 tests in SOA.
  • Training (What you need to learn): Continual education seminars to hold onto certification Be familiar with programming, presenting, finance, and have strong problem-solving skills
  • Experience (Any job experience prior?): Internships to improve your chances of getting a job
  • Pay: Median annual wage of $97,000
  • Job Trend: 11% growth between 2014-2024
  • Emerging: Actuarial analysts are in high demands because many insurance companies need them to analyze large amounts of information that come from the consumers.
  • Threatened by AI?: Actuaries would not be replaced by AI because actuaries require the thinking of worst case scenarios in which current AI can not do. AI can not currently do any critical thinking and won't be able to for quite a while.
