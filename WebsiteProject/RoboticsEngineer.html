Robotics Engineer

Description: A robotics engineer helps to create and program robots that perform tasks too dangerous or unhealthy for a normal human to do. They use computer-aided design and drafting as well as computer-aided manufacturing to create these robots. Robotics engineers can also design systems and research for ways to produce the robots in a more economic efficient manner.

  • Education Required: Bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or related field
  • Training (What you need to learn): coursework for a robot engineering degree includes advanced math, physical and life sciences, computer science, computer-aided design and drafting, physics, fluid dynamics and materials science
  • Experience (Any job experience prior?): Exposure to physical science and mathematics
  • Pay: Median wage of $89,590
  • Job Trend: 5% growth (2014-2024)
  • Emerging: Female engineers are in very high demand so this would be a good, emerging career for aspiring women.
  • Threatened by AI?: Robotic engineers can not be replaced until an ai learns how program and make a more efficient robot.
